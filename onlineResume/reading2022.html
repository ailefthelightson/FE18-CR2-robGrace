Personal Reading List 2022


This was a varied year, topic-wise. I was doing full time law school with all the reading this entails and threw myself, at the same time, into learning the agricultural system of William Albrecht. Less time for my usual rabbit-holing, with case-law, policy white papers and mineral requirements of higher level plants chewing up my reading bandwidth.



Albrecht on Calcium (The Albrecht Papers)

The king of nutrients, calcium plays its amazing role in soil fertility. Always amazed how its calculated applications effects other mineral content percentages on top of all the other physiological processes it enables.



The Anatomy of Life and Energy in Agriculture

Arden Andersen reshapes my understanding of soil and plants - a book taken in brief sips requiring ample digestion time.



Soil Science for Gardeners

A basic introduction into navigating the complex world of soil. Boils down complex subjects for beginners like me to understand, upon which a plan of action can be confidently built.



The Potato Crop

Life in Austria is sustained by the potato and the pig. Great read to learn mineral requirements of your specific varietal and best practices for your farming season.



The Rodale Book of Composting

The bible of composting. Granular detail, reduced complexity, no topic stone left unturned. A must-have for every aerobic microbe farmer.



A World Beneath the Sands: The Golden Age of Egyptology

A book I can relax with, told talk-story style covering all the interesting happenings in this amazing part of the world for the last 200 years.



American Hemp Farmer: Adventures and Misadventures in the Cannabis Trade

Doug Fine is restorative agriculture's Kurt Vonnegut, tho none of his pursuits are fictional in substance, only absurd in outcome. Light, funny, life-affirming stuff.



Mao's Great Famine: The History of China's Most Devastating Catastrophe, 1958-1962

Never hurts to look back on the folly of how large-scale social experiments played out in the twentieth century. Democide, famine, misery, state over-reach - all the hallmarks of our current condition we don't want to accept exists.



Wrap Contracts: Foundations and Ramifications

Digital contract law and the imbalance of equity between customer and merchant. Unpacking the reality of every "agree" button I've ever clicked.



Sacred and Herbal Healing beginners

Upper Austria is to beer what Lower Austria is to wine. A good primer for the optimal homebrew product.



Propaganda

Eye-rollingly fed up with people still believing their governments actually care about them, this is the seminal, post-Goebbels propaganda bible to unwind the slavish acceptance of broadcast media's claim on veracity.
